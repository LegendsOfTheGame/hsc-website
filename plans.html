// Questions

Fair Questions, Straight Answers

Why pay monthly instead of just calling when there's a problem?

Because by the time you notice the problem, your customers noticed it first. Tags multiply when they sit — a wall that gets cleaned fast stays clean. Regular care also keeps you ahead of Hamilton's property standards bylaw (10-118), which requires graffiti-free exteriors. And that matters: once someone reports your wall and a bylaw notice lands, you have 13 days to clean it — or the City sends its own crew and bills you for the work. A weekly visit means it never gets that far.

How do I know you actually showed up?

Every visit is GPS-logged and timestamped in our field system the moment it happens, and you get a photo report after each visit. This is the same documentation standard we use on our BIA contracts.

Is there a contract or minimum term?

No. Month to month, cancel anytime with a quick email. We keep customers by being useful, not by locking them in.

What if my building gets hit with major graffiti?

Small tags and stickers are included in every plan. Large-scale pieces are quoted separately — and that's exactly where the City's grant kicks in: up to 50% of the invoice back (max $200 per incident, 5 times a year), and we help you file for it.

How fast can you remove graffiti?

Straight answer: it depends on the method. A paint-over (matched to your wall colour) is fast — on the Twice-a-Week plan it's usually handled by the very next visit. Full chemical removal is scheduled by surface type and weather, because we work with commercial-grade remover, not a pressure washer — so we won't promise next-day removal we can't deliver. You always get a real timeline up front.

Who pays for the paint on a paint-over?

Often nobody. We keep a stock of common paint colours in our storage specifically for quick paint-overs — if one of ours matches your wall, that keeps your cost down. If your wall needs a proper colour match, we buy the paint at our discounted rate; you pay the paint cost, and the can is yours to keep afterwards for future touch-ups.

Do you serve my street?

We're based in Hamilton's lower city — Barton Village and Ottawa Street are home turf — and serve surrounding commercial corridors. Outside the core? Ask anyway; if we can't make it work, we'll say so.
