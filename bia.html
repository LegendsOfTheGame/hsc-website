// What a Contract Covers

The Full Streetscape,
Handled

  • Litter & debris removal — sidewalks, tree pits, curb lines, and the alley mouths everyone else skips.
  • Graffiti response — rapid removal on member storefronts and street furniture, plus outreach to owners about the City's grant program.
  • Cleanliness monitoring & reporting — a standing property-by-property picture of your corridor: what's tagged, what's overgrown, what needs a City service request.
  • Beautification support — planters, seasonal installs, parkette upkeep, and event-day cleanups.
  • City liaison legwork — we file the Report-a-Problem tickets (dumping, needles, dead trees) with photos and GPS, so issues actually get actioned.
// Why HSC

Small Crew,
Operations-Grade

  • Proven on Barton Street. We hold the street maintenance contract for Barton Village BIA and monitor 80+ properties on the corridor, storefront by storefront.
  • Committee-level fluency. We sit on Ottawa Street BIA's Streetscape & Beautification Committee — we speak board, budget, and levy, not just broom.
  • Data, not vibes. Every visit is GPS-logged and timestamped in our own field system. Your weekly report is generated from real records, not memory.

// Engagement Models

Three Ways to Engage

Seasonal Contract

Fixed weekly hours across your season (e.g. April–October), invoiced predictably, scoped to your budget line. The model we run today on Barton Street.

Corridor Audit

A one-time, property-by-property condition report of your corridor — graffiti, waste, bylaw exposure — with photos and a prioritized action list. A board-ready baseline before you commit to anything.

Overflow & Events

Already have a cleaner? We handle surge work: event-day cleanups, graffiti backlogs, construction-season dust-ups, and vacation coverage.
