Your storefront gets graffitied on a Wednesday. By Thursday morning, you're stressed. By Friday afternoon, it's gone — photographed, documented, and handled. That's not luck. That's what a care plan looks like in practice.

Here's how it actually works, using Lakeside Books as a real example.

## The Intake Conversation

Before we ever schedule the first visit, we ask questions:

- What are your hours of operation?
- Do you have a day you're closed?
- What's your preferred visit day?

This matters because we don't want to interfere with your business. But if Lakeside Books closes on Thursdays, that Thursday becomes our day to visit. When you're not there, we're the eyes on the ground — checking for overnight damage, vandalism, graffiti, or break-ins. Think of it like unpaid security coverage built into the service.

## Thursday Visit: The Inspection

We arrive at Lakeside Books on Thursday morning. The first thing we do: **record the exact time we arrived** in our field notes.

Then we walk.

We start with **litter and the perimeter** — bag and picker in hand. We check the plants and trees around the storefront. We pick up debris, cigarette butts, anything that's accumulated since the last visit. This takes about **15–20 minutes** depending on what we find.

While we're there, we do a **full visual inspection**:

- Front entrance and side walk
- Windows and doors (looking for stickers, marks, damage)
- Signage (any new tags or vandalism?)
- Evidence of break-in attempts
- General condition of the storefront

This is where we're looking for exactly what we found at Lakeside Books on this particular Thursday: **graffiti on the door**.

### We Found Graffiti

There it was — writing on the emergency exit door. White paint on black.

We **photograph it immediately** (before photo), take note of the location, and document everything in our field system. Then we assess: Can we remove this today with what we have on hand?

On this day, we didn't have black paint with us. So here's what happens next:

**We notify Lakeside Books right away.**

We let them know what we found, where it is, and that we're going to take care of it. Lakeside Books asks us to come back Friday afternoon and paint over it — the sooner the better.

That's the last thing we do on Thursday: **5 minutes of on-site documentation**, then we're done.

**Thursday total time on site: 20–25 minutes.**

## Friday Visit: The Paint-Over Response

We return Friday afternoon with:
- Black paint (matched to their door colour, from our storage)
- A wet paint sign (so customers don't accidentally touch it)
- Time to do it right

We spend **25 minutes ensuring complete coverage**. We're not rushing. Tags can bleed through if you don't apply the paint properly, so we take our time, paint carefully, and make sure the coverage is solid.

Once the paint is dry, we **photograph the cleaned area** (after photo).

## The Documentation

Both photos — before and after — upload directly to a **shared Google Photos album** that Lakeside Books has access to 24/7. They can pull it up on their phone anytime. They can see everything we've done. They can track patterns if graffiti keeps hitting the same spot. They have **complete visibility into their property**, no waiting for a report, no email they might miss.

This is also how we prove the work was done: GPS timestamp, photo proof, date and time recorded in our field system.

## Why This Matters

Here's what Lakeside Books got out of this:

1. **Peace of mind** — graffiti was spotted and handled fast. Tags don't sit around multiplying.
2. **Photo proof** — they have documented evidence that their storefront is being maintained.
3. **Compliance** — they stay ahead of Hamilton's [Property Standards Bylaw (10-118)](https://live-city-of-hamilton.pantheonsite.io/sites/default/files/2025-12/10-118-consolidated-nov-2025.pdf), which requires graffiti-free exteriors. If the City comes by and finds a clean storefront, there's no bylaw notice, no 13-day cleanup deadline, no City crew billing them for removal work.
4. **Professionalism** — they run a bookstore. They don't run a graffiti removal business. We do.
5. **The grant** — if the removal cost exceeds what they pay monthly on their care plan, we suggest filing for the City's grant to cover up to 50% of the removal cost (max $200 per incident, up to 5 times per year). We help them file for it.

## The Real Difference: Consistency

The thing most people miss: a storefront that gets cleaned fast stays clean. Graffiti is territorial. If a tagger sees their work gets removed within 24 hours, they move on to a wall that'll stay tagged longer.

A storefronts that's hit once a month and cleaned once a month? That wall becomes a target. A storefront that's walked every week, inspected every week, with graffiti removed before it becomes visible? That wall stops being worth the tagger's time.

Consistency is the difference between a clean storefront and constant graffiti.

## What You Actually Get

A care plan means:

- Regular inspections on a schedule you choose
- Litter and debris pickup
- Small tag and sticker removal
- Photo documentation of every visit
- A shared album showing your property 24/7
- Graffiti response (either immediate removal or next-visit paint-over, depending on what we find)
- Grant paperwork help when a big hit happens
- One phone number, one consistent person, no surprises

No contracts. No minimums. Month to month, cancel anytime.

Because we keep customers by being useful, not by locking them in.
