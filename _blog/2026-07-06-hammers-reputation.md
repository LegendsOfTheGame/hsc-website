We started the day wondering if the rain would find us. The forecast called for 23.6 degrees, a 7% chance of precipitation, and a friendly reminder to grab our coffee from Eldercamp — a long-term sponsor, and our weekly breakfast ritual when we're in the field.

Not even 100 meters down the road, we found a full garbage bag worth of clothing piled on the street just off Barnesdale. Thanks to our friends at Helping Hands Street Mission and their free store, we bagged it all up. We noticed an umbrella in there — hopefully it finds someone who needs it when the rain comes. By the end of the week, we know all that clothing will be washed and ready for community members who need it.

Down the street, we noticed Staropolskie Delikatesy (711 Barton St E) was closed for the week — they're being used as a film set for a Christmas movie. Merry Christmas in July, eh?

## The Memorial

We also found a memorial on an abandoned door — a community member had written a message about someone they knew, someone the neighbourhood had lost. We learned that JH, a person from the community, had passed away, likely due to heat-related complications during the heat advisory that gripped Hamilton all of last week. The memorial was left by people who knew JH, people who cared.

We made a personal decision to let that sit for a week. We didn't know JH, but we give all community members the respect they deserve — in life and in loss. So we left it. On Monday, we came back and cleaned it respectfully, as a quiet closure to a week of remembrance.

## The Numbers

Our Monday pass covered the full 4.25 km route through Barton Village. 

We collected 2.78 bags of garbage — that's roughly 22 kilograms of litter. Lighter than usual, which was fantastic to see.

## Route Change

We had planned to work all the way to Ferguson, but we cut the route short at Victoria to attend a meeting about the Hands On Barton project, which launches later this week. That meeting took priority, so we shifted our timing and doubled back through Woodlands Park on the return.

## What We Found in Woodlands Park

That's where things got serious.

We found needles and drug paraphernalia — drug pipes — literally meters from the splash pad. Kids use that splash pad. Families take their children there. And right now, it's not safe. That's not acceptable.

We also encountered the park bathroom situation. The smell was unbearable. We chatted with a community member who had to literally hold their breath and plug their nose just to use the urinal. From a meter and a half away, with the door open, we could smell it too. 

That person said what we've been thinking: the park needs on-site security. They need maintenance. They need accountability for public health and safety.

Here's the thing: Edmonton already does this. The City of Edmonton contracts with companies like [HireGood](https://linkly.link/2m7Gn) to station 2 people outside park washrooms 24/7. It works. And Hamilton? According to [Ward 3 City Councillor candidate Andrew Selman](https://linkly.link/2m7Hu), we hired 1,000+ new city staff over the last four years — while spending $150 million on external consultants. So the workforce exists. The question is priorities. Why can't 2 out of 1,000 be stationed here, preventing someone from smoking meth in a family park?

## What We're Tracking for Friday

Here's why this matters right now: Hamilton's Mayor is coming to Helping Hands Street Mission on Friday, July 10th, from 1:30 PM to 4:00 PM. She's there to discuss with the community — not just community members, but all of us — about social housing, shelters, waste collection changes, road maintenance, parks and recreation, public transit and LRT changes, police matters, and the future of our library.

We have data to contribute to that conversation.

## Transit Vandalism — The Real Cost

Every shift, we spend **20 to 25 minutes cleaning transit infrastructure alone.** That's roughly 20–25 minutes out of every 5-hour day dedicated to removing graffiti and vandalism from bus shelters, transit signage, and related infrastructure. We spend approximately **$3 in supplies per shift** just on that work.

For us, that's time and money that could go elsewhere. It's a real operational cost to keep transit spaces from becoming unusable public spaces.

## This Week

Hands On Barton launches later this week. The mayor visits Friday. We're planning a full graffiti audit on Thursday. And we're carrying all of this — the memorial we respected, the clothing we collected, the park conditions we documented, the transit impact we're quantifying — into conversations about what Hamilton's streets should be and who gets to have a role in keeping them safe and clean.

We're not just maintaining Barton Village. We're observing, documenting, and bringing community data to the people who make decisions about our neighbourhood.

Hamilton will decide what **The Hammer's Reputation** is. Look what you made me do, and we will keep giving them something worth talking about
